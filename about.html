One of the first rappers at the beginning of the hip hop period close to the end of the 70's was hip hop's first DJ, DJ Kool Herc. Herc was a Jamaican immigrant who started delivering simple raps at his parties, rapping was inspired by the Jamaican tradition of "toasting"

The rythmic flow and lyrical genius found a life during the 90's which is known as the "Golden Age" of hip hop. Rappers put their lives into their lyrics and spoke about real problems that faced them. From the communities they grew up in to the goals for their future, 90's rappers held nothing back. Their raw flow and style is what continues to make it's legacy so iconic decades and decades later. The legendary N.W.A group brought a brazen and unapolegetic style to the music industry and rapped about police brutality, systemic racism and ultimately cultre

Street Culture look many forms during the 90's from clothes to language to film. As culture expanded and evolved the modern rap culture we have today is strong because of our past. The beats and Content may have changed over the years but one universal theme is "The Come Up".The come up is story behind the words, the reason why rappers rap, to have a better life for their families, to inspire the youth who listen or to strive higher to schieve their ultimate goal. Modern visionaries like A$AP Rocky and the A$AP Mob, J.Cole, Kendrick Lamar, Drake, Young M.A. and many more use their platform to candidly create Art and Life.
